### **1. Hypertension**  
- **Symptoms**: Often asymptomatic; headaches, dizziness, shortness of breath in severe cases.  
- **Demographics**: Age >45 years; males > females pre-menopause; higher prevalence in Black populations.  
- **Medications**: Thiazide diuretics (e.g., hydrochlorothiazide), ACE inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril).  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - **Pheochromocytoma** (paroxysmal hypertension, headache, sweating)   
  - **Renal artery stenosis** (abdominal bruit, refractory hypertension)  
  - **Hyperaldosteronism** (hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Chest pain, dyspnea, visual disturbances (hypertensive emergency)  
  - Weight loss, palpitations, diaphoresis (secondary causes like pheochromocytoma)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: I10 (Essential (primary) hypertension)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Basic metabolic panel (electrolytes, creatinine), lipid panel, urinalysis, EKG .

### **2. Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus**  
- **Symptoms**: Polyuria, polydipsia, fatigue, blurred vision.  
- **Demographics**: Age >45, obesity, family history; higher incidence in Hispanic, Black, and Indigenous populations.  
- **Medications**: Metformin (first-line), GLP-1 receptor agonists, insulin.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - **Type 1 diabetes** (acute onset, ketosis-prone, younger age)   
  - **Secondary diabetes** (Cushing’s syndrome, hemochromatosis)  
  - **Impaired glucose tolerance** (HbA1c 5.7–6.4%)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Unintentional weight loss, polyuria at night, recurrent infections (risk of complications)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: E11.9 (Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Fasting glucose, HbA1c, urine microalbumin, lipid panel .

### **3. Upper Respiratory Tract Infection (URTI)**  
- **Symptoms**: Sore throat, rhinorrhea, cough, low-grade fever.  
- **Demographics**: All ages; peaks in children and elderly. Seasonal variation noted.  
- **Medications**: Symptomatic (e.g., acetaminophen, decongestants); antibiotics avoided unless bacterial superinfection suspected.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - **Influenza** (systemic myalgia, high fever)   
  - **Streptococcal pharyngitis** (exudates, tender lymphadenopathy)  
  - **Allergic rhinitis** (clear rhinorrhea, itchy eyes)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Dyspnea, stridor, drooling (suspect epiglottitis or peritonsillar abscess)   
  - Persistent fever >5 days, neck stiffness (consider meningitis)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: J06.9 (Acute upper respiratory infection, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Rapid strep test, influenza swab (if indicated), chest X-ray for suspected pneumonia .

### **4. Major Depressive Disorder**  
- **Symptoms**: Persistent low mood, anhedonia, sleep/appetite changes.  
- **Demographics**: Age 25–49 peak; females > males (2:1 ratio).  
- **Medications**: SSRIs (e.g., sertraline), SNRIs; psychotherapy adjunctive.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - **Bipolar disorder** (history of manic episodes)   
  - **Hypothyroidism** (fatigue, weight gain)   
  - **Anemia** (fatigue, pallor)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Suicidal ideation, self-harm, psychosis (require urgent psychiatric referral)   
  - Unexplained weight loss, fatigue (rule out organic causes)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: F32.9 (Major depressive disorder, single episode, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - TSH, CBC, vitamin D, B12 levels to exclude organic causes .

### **5. Osteoarthritis**  
- **Symptoms**: Joint pain, stiffness, crepitus.  
- **Demographics**: Age >60; postmenopausal women, athletes.  
- **Medications**: NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen), acetaminophen, intra-articular corticosteroids.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - **Rheumatoid arthritis** (morning stiffness >1 hour, symmetrical joints)   
  - **Gout** (acute monoarticular arthritis, erythema)   
  - **Fibromyalgia** (widespread pain, tender points)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Joint swelling, warmth, erythema (suspect septic arthritis or gout)   
  - Unexplained weight loss, fever (consider malignancy or infection)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: M19.90 (Osteoarthritis, unspecified joint)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - ESR/CRP (inflammation), synovial fluid analysis for crystals or infection .

### **6. Rheumatoid Arthritis**  
- **Symptoms**: Morning stiffness (>1 hour), joint swelling, fatigue.  
- **Demographics**: Age 30–60; females > males (3:1).  
- **Medications**: Methotrexate (first-line), NSAIDs, biologics.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Osteoarthritis (crepitus, non-inflammatory)  
  - Systemic lupus erythematosus (malar rash, photosensitivity)  
  - Gout (acute monoarticular attacks)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Joint deformities, subcutaneous nodules (advanced disease)  
  - Unexplained weight loss, fever (suspect malignancy or infection)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: M06.9 (Rheumatoid arthritis, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - RF, anti-CCP antibodies, ESR/CRP, X-rays/ultrasound of affected joints.

### **7. Migraine**  
- **Symptoms**: Pulsating headache, photophobia, nausea.  
- **Demographics**: Age 18–44, females > males (3:1).  
- **Medications**: Triptans (e.g., sumatriptan), prophylactic beta-blockers (e.g., propranolol).  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Tension-type headache (bilateral, non-pulsatile)  
  - Cluster headache (orbital pain, autonomic symptoms)  
  - Secondary headaches (e.g., subarachnoid hemorrhage, meningitis)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Thunderclap headache (suspect aneurysm)  
  - Focal neurological deficits (e.g., hemiparesis, visual field loss)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: G43.909 (Migraine without aura, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - CT/MRI head (if red flags present), ESR for giant cell arteritis (age >50).

### **8. Epilepsy**  
- **Symptoms**: Seizures, loss of consciousness, post-ictal confusion.  
- **Demographics**: Pediatric or elderly onset.  
- **Medications**: Levetiracetam, lamotrigine, valproic acid.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Syncope (brief loss of consciousness, no post-ictal phase)  
  - Psychogenic non-epileptic seizures (normal EEG)  
  - Hypoglycemia (responsive to glucose)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Status epilepticus (>5 minutes of continuous seizure)  
  - New-onset seizures in adults >50 years (rule out tumor/stroke)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: G40.909 (Epilepsy, unspecified, not intractable)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - EEG, MRI brain, serum glucose, toxicology screen.

### **9. Hypothyroidism**  
- **Symptoms**: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance.  
- **Demographics**: Females > males; age >60.  
- **Medications**: Levothyroxine.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Depression (anhedonia, sleep disturbances)  
  - Anemia (pallor, low hemoglobin)  
  - Chronic fatigue syndrome (prolonged fatigue without lab abnormalities)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Bradycardia, hypothermia (myxedema coma)  
  - Hoarseness, goiter (suspect thyroid cancer)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: E03.9 (Hypothyroidism, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - TSH, free T4, lipid panel (elevated cholesterol).  

---

### **10. Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)**  
- **Symptoms**: Menstrual irregularity, hirsutism, acne.  
- **Demographics**: Reproductive-age females.  
- **Medications**: Oral contraceptives, metformin.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Thyroid dysfunction (fatigue, weight changes)  
  - Hyperprolactinemia (galactorrhea, amenorrhea)  
  - Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (virilization)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Rapid weight gain, striae (suspect Cushing’s syndrome)  
  - Pelvic mass (rule out ovarian tumors)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: E28.2 (Polycystic ovary syndrome)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - LH/FSH ratio, testosterone, TSH, fasting glucose.

### **11. Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)**  
- **Symptoms**: Itchy, erythematous, scaly patches.  
- **Demographics**: Pediatric onset; adults with dry skin.  
- **Medications**: Topical corticosteroids, emollients.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Contact dermatitis (allergic/irritant triggers)  
  - Psoriasis (silvery plaques, scalp/nails involvement)  
  - Scabies (burrows, intense pruritus at night)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Erythroderma (widespread erythema, systemic toxicity)  
  - Secondary infection (pus, fever)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: L20.9 (Atopic dermatitis, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Skin scraping (fungal infection), patch testing (allergens).  

---

### **12. Psoriasis**  
- **Symptoms**: Silvery plaques, joint pain (psoriatic arthritis).  
- **Demographics**: Age 15–35; family history.  
- **Medications**: Topical steroids, methotrexate, biologics.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Seborrheic dermatitis (greasy scales, scalp/folds)  
  - Lichen planus (violaceous papules, Wickham’s striae)  
  - Secondary syphilis (painless rash, systemic symptoms)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Arthritis mutilans (destructive joint disease)  
  - Pustular psoriasis (fever, widespread pustules)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: L40.9 (Psoriasis, unspecified)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Skin biopsy, rheumatoid factor (if arthritis suspected).

### **13. Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)**  
- **Symptoms**: Urinary frequency, weak stream, nocturia.  
- **Demographics**: Males >50 years.  
- **Medications**: Alpha-blockers (e.g., tamsulosin), 5-alpha reductase inhibitors (e.g., finasteride).  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Prostate cancer (asymptomatic nodule on DRE)  
  - Urinary tract infection (dysuria, urgency)  
  - Neurogenic bladder (history of spinal cord injury)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Hematuria, bone pain (suspect prostate cancer)  
  - Acute urinary retention (painful distended bladder)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: N40.1 (Benign prostatic hyperplasia with lower urinary tract symptoms)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - PSA, urinalysis, renal function tests.

### **14. Breast Cancer Screening**  
- **Symptoms**: Breast lump, nipple discharge, skin dimpling.  
- **Demographics**: Females >40; BRCA mutation carriers.  
- **Medications**: Mammography (screening); hormonal therapy (e.g., tamoxifen) for prevention.  
- **Differential Diagnoses**:  
  - Fibroadenoma (mobile, painless mass)  
  - Cyst (fluctuant, tender)  
  - Mastitis (erythema, warmth, lactation-associated)   
- **Red-Flag Symptoms**:  
  - Fixed, irregular mass (high suspicion for malignancy)  
  - Unilateral nipple discharge (especially bloody)   
- **ICD-10 Code**: Z12.31 (Encounter for screening mammogram for malignant neoplasm of breast)  
- **Laboratory Investigations**:  
  - Mammogram, ultrasound, biopsy (if suspicious lesion).
